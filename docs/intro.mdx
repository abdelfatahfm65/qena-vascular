# Vascular Surgery

## Conceptual Framework

Vascular surgery deals with disorders that compromise **arterial inflow**, **venous outflow**, or **vessel wall integrity**. Rather than isolated diseases, vascular conditions are best understood as disturbances in **blood flow dynamics**, leading to ischemia, venous hypertension, or hemorrhage.

---

## Anatomical Basis

### Arterial System
Arterial pathology primarily affects tissue perfusion. Clinically, this manifests as:
- **Acute limb ischemia** due to sudden arterial occlusion
- **Chronic limb-threatening ischemia** from progressive atherosclerosis
- **Aneurysmal disease** resulting from degeneration of the arterial wall

### Venous System
Venous disorders arise from impaired venous return and valvular dysfunction, leading to:
- **Varicose veins and chronic venous insufficiency**
- **Deep vein thrombosis**
- **Arteriovenous fistulas**, either congenital or acquired

---

## Clinical Correlation

Acute vascular conditions require **urgent assessment and intervention** to preserve life and limb, whereas chronic conditions demand **systematic evaluation and long-term management**. Accurate diagnosis relies on integrating **anatomical knowledge**, **pathophysiology**, and **clinical presentation**.
